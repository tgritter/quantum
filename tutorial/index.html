Quantum Simulator Tutorial

Part 1: Introduction

Welcome to the wonderful world of Quantum Computing! In this tutorial series I will be going over some cool features of how quantum computing works. I have labeled each section and given a brief explaination of what the video tutorials will be discussing. My favourite section is the one of the Universal Quantum Computer, so if you're going to just watch one I recommended that one.





Part 2: Qubits

In this section I will explain what the qubit is and how it differs from a classical bit. We will be working with the simulator to explain the curiours pheonema of superposition.





Part 3: Quantum Gates

In this section I will be using the similuator to show the what the effects quantum gates have on the Qubit. We will see that these gates can perform certain operations that normal gates cannot.





Part 4: Deutsch-Jonza Algorithm

In this section I will be exploring the first algorithm to show the power that Quantum Computing has. It's designing specifically for a quantum computer and really shows the benefits of using superposition.





Part 5: Grover's Algorithm

In this section I will explore an algorithm that can performed a search much faster than a classical computer. It has very important applications for database theory.





Part 6: Quantum Computer vs Simulators

In this section I will be exploring the benefits that an actual quantum computer has over the simulator we've been using. I will also be exploring the progress that some pretty cool quantum computing companies have been doing.





Part 7: Universal Quantum Computing

This is my favourite topic as it explores how viewing the Universal Turing Machine as a instance of a Quantum computer. This has some profound affects about how we should look at physics, computing and the nature of the universe.
