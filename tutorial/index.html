Quantum Simulator Tutorial

Part 1: Introduction

Welcome to the wonderful world of Quantum Computing! In this tutorial series I will be going over the key features of quantum computing by examining some of the key equations and seeing what they compute using a quantum simulator. I have labeled each section and given a brief explaination of what the video tutorials will be discussing. By the end I hope you will be able to appreciate the incredible potential that quantum computing has!





Part 2: Qubits

In this section I will explain what the qubit is and how it differs from a classical bit. We will be working with the simulator to explain the curiours pheonema of superposition. I will be also be exploring the 'Beam Spilter' experiment to illustrate what it means for a qubit to be in parallel universes at the sametime.





Part 3: Quantum Gates

In this section I will be using the similuator to show the effects that applying Quantum Gates to qubits has. I will go over the Bloch Sphere and how it allows us to better represent how a qubit changes within a quantum state.





Part 4: Deutsch-Jozsa Algorithm

In this section I will be exploring the first algorithm to show how quantum computers are able to compute functions faster than a classical computer is able to. I will first exmaine a few examples of the algorithm, and then use the simulator to explain excatly how a quantum computer is able to solve it faster.





Part 5: Grover's Algorithm

In this section I will be exploring a quantum algorithm for unstructured searches that has a much faster runtime that a classical algorithm. I will explain how this algorithm using amplitude amplification to increase the probably of observing the correct output.





Part 6: Quantum Computer vs Simulators

In this section I will be exploring the differences between Quantum Computers and Simulators. I explain how quantum computers require less computational complexity as a result making calculations in parallel universes, but are also susceptible to the problem of decoherence. I also explore some of the latest developments of some very interesting quantum computing companies.





Part 7: Universal Quantum Computing

This is my favourite subject within quantum computing as it explores the connection between computing and the structure of the universe. I will be exploring David Deustch's paper to explain how viewing the outputs of Turing machines as physical states of the universe shows how it possible to create physical states that do not otherwise exist in nature. I also explore the idea how the Theory of Computing informs us how we think in everyday life.
